Sunswift is entirely student-led, not-for-profit and volunteer-based. Sunswift relies on donations and sponsorships to continue operating. If you would like to make a contribution to Sunswift, please click here.

We couldn’t attain the heights of success without the help of our great sponsors.

PLATINUM

UNSW Faculty of Engineering has been one of our greatest supporters since our inception. The UNSW Faculty of Engineering is ranked 33rd in the QS World University Rankings for Engineering and Technology. UNSW is the most awarded university in Australia according to the World Universities Guide and UNSW graduates have the highest median salaries of any university in Australia.

GOLD

Core Builders assisted significantly in the construction of eVe’s carbon fibre body. Core Builders have received international recognition for their exceptional composites and building materials. In addition to the support they have provided us with they have worked on such projects as the America’s Cup AC45 Catamarans.

Thales is renowned for being at the cutting-edge of technology. Naturally, we see eye-to-eye in reaching the limits of innovation. Thales have helped significantly with provision of supplies and giving expert advice to our project.

Nova Systems assisted with the implementation of systems engineering into the construction of eVe. Their mentorship took our team’s engineering process to the next level.

Sunswift eVe is powered by Sunpower Solar Cells. Sunpower have donated their high-efficiency mono-crystalline solar cells which are among the most efficient commercially available silicon solar cells in the world.

Solbian have supported Sunswift with their solar cell encapsulation solutions. Using Solbian's encapsulation technology, Sunswift's solar array is resistant to damage from the harsh conditions of the Australian outback - all without compromising efficiency or weight.

SILVER

Lavender have partnered with Sunswift, offering creative and technological assistance with our online content and marketing strategy.

Leap Australia were a huge part in the creation of eVe, offering professional expertise and training in computational fluid dynamics and aerodynamics design.

Mo Milling is a Sydney-based company who have helped Sunswift to create custom parts with technological expertise and machining services.

3M is a brand recognised and trusted around the globe that produces various products and services. 3M have been helpful to Sunswift by providing the team with valuable adhesives.

Addcom Contact Solutions supported UNSW’s Sunwift in the 2011 World Solar Challenge. With their help, Sunswift was able to utilise Addcom’s satellite communications in the Australian Outback.

Graphique Solutions is an Australian owned signage company. They have helped us with many of our printing and signage needs.

Seiko are the official timekeepers of the Sunswift team. Seiko assisted us in breaking the Guinness World Record for the fastest solar powered vehicle in 2011.

Weatherzone supported Sunswift with their highly-accurate weather forecasting services. With their help, Sunswift were able to determine weather conditions which assisted in making decisions during the World Solar Challenge and the 2009 Global Green Challenge.

Oxeon’s TeXtreme carbon composites deliver ultra-lightness and stiffness above that of conventional carbon fibre fabrics. Oxeon assisted Sunswift by suppling the team with the carbon fibre composites that make up eVe’s body.

Google Australia have provided financial assistance to Sunswift as well as professional expertise with programming.

Comply Serve have partnered with Sunswift to assist with aerodynamics design using their computational fluid dynamics software and professional training.
